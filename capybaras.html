The capybara or greater capybara (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) is a giant cavy rodent native to South America. It is the largest living rodent. Capybaras are also known as the "coconut dog."



Capybaras have been shown to be extremely friendly to most other animal species.




Capybaras are semiaquatic animals, which means they can move quite well on land and swim just as well. They can stay submerged for up to 5 minutes.
